# The Sync Maximalist - Research Notes

## Overview
Leaders who believe everything must be a meeting and cannot communicate asynchronously. This creates calendar gridlock, meeting fatigue, and prevents deep work.

## Key Research & Sources

- **77% of respondents** say meetings only create more meetings
- **44% of workers** dread meetings

**Microsoft Work Trend Index**

## The Pattern Description

The Sync Maximalist manager:
- Schedules meetings to discuss things that could be a document
- Requires real-time attendance for one-way information sharing
- Uses meetings for decisions that don't need everyone present
- Creates meeting chains: meeting to plan meeting to debrief meeting
- Believes visibility = value (if they can't see you working, you aren't)

Common symptoms:
- "Let's hop on a quick call" for every question
- Daily or multiple standups
- Meetings scheduled during focus blocks
- No written documentation of decisions

## Why It Hurts

1. **Calendar Tetris**: No time blocks large enough for deep work
2. **Meeting Recovery**: Each meeting needs ~23 min recovery time
3. **Overtime Required**: 51% work overtime just to catch up on real work
4. **Burnout**: 76% feel drained after meeting-heavy days
5. **Documentation Death**: Decisions made in meetings are lost or forgotten
6. **Timezone Exclusion**: Sync-heavy culture punishes distributed teams

## The Fix

1. **Default to Async**: Write a document, Slack message, or Loom video first
2. **Meeting Audit**: For each recurring meeting, ask "Could this be async?"
3. **Agendas Required**: No agenda, no meeting
4. **Shorter Defaults**: 25 or 50 minutes instead of 30 or 60
5. **Meeting-Free Days**: Designate days (e.g., Tues/Thurs) with no meetings
6. **Document Decisions**: Every meeting produces written outcomes
7. **Office Hours**: Replace 1:1 requests with scheduled availability

## If You're Affected

1. **Decline with Alternatives**: "I can respond async - can you share this in Slack?"
2. **Block Focus Time**: Put "deep work" blocks on your calendar
3. **Request Agendas**: Ask for agenda before accepting
4. **Suggest Async Tools**: Loom for demos, docs for decisions
5. **Calculate the Cost**: Show your manager the productivity math
6. **Propose Experiments**: "Let's try async standups for two weeks"
